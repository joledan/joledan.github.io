The Republic of India is building buzz in the international community. Recent dinner invitations to G20 members refer to President Droupadi Murmu as the "President of Bharat." If India seriously considers a name change, it will join the ranks of countries that have experienced the process of "re-branding."

<!--more-->

![fig1]({{site.url}}/assets/images/wiki-nc/wiki-nc-fig1.png)

[This Wikipedia article](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geographical_renaming) lists significant country name changes from the 1500s to present day. Name changes can occur due to country break-ups, such as the fall of the Soviet Union in the 1990s. Renaming is also an act of decolonization: around 31 ex-European colonies changed their names from 1940 to 1980. 

Earlier this year The Republic of Türkiye became the latest country to officially change its name, previously known as The Republic of Turkey. Perhaps there will be a new name to remember.
